title: Fill Pricing
description: How HyPaper computes realistic fill prices using VWAP

## Overview

HyPaper doesn't fill orders at the mid price. It computes a **Volume-Weighted Average Price (VWAP)** by walking the real HyperLiquid L2 order book. This simulates realistic slippage based on order size and book depth.

## How VWAP works

When a buy order fills, HyPaper walks the **ask side** of the book (lowest to highest). For sells, it walks the **bid side** (highest to lowest).

### Example: buying 3 BTC

Given this ask book:

| Level | Price | Size |
|-------|-------|------|
| 1 | $50,000 | 1.0 |
| 2 | $50,010 | 2.0 |
| 3 | $50,020 | 3.0 |

Filling 3 BTC:
- 1.0 @ $50,000 = $50,000
- 2.0 @ $50,010 = $100,020
- **Total**: $150,020 / 3.0 = **$50,006.67**

The VWAP is $50,006.67 — slightly worse than the best ask, reflecting realistic slippage.

## Limit price clamping

The VWAP walk stops at your limit price. Levels beyond the limit are skipped. This means:

- **Buy VWAP is never above your limit price**
- **Sell VWAP is never below your limit price**

You always get a price equal to or better than your limit.

## Insufficient book depth

If the order book doesn't have enough depth to fill the entire order, the remaining quantity is priced at the **worst available level** (clamped to the limit price).

### Example: buying 10 BTC with only 6 BTC of depth

| Book | Priced at book levels |
|------|----------------------|
| 6 BTC filled from 3 levels | VWAP of those levels |
| 4 BTC remaining | Priced at last level ($50,020), clamped to limit |

## Fallback pricing

If the L2 book is unavailable (network issues, cache miss), the order fills at the **mid price**. This is the conservative fallback — no slippage simulation.

## L2 book cache

To avoid hammering the HyperLiquid API, L2 books are cached for **2 seconds** with request deduplication. Concurrent fills for the same coin share a single book fetch.

## Market orders (triggers)

Trigger orders with `isMarket: true` use the mid price as the base but still apply VWAP. There's no limit price clamping — the fill price reflects the actual book depth at the trigger moment.
